## Chapter 28: Martial Law

### Scene 1: The Reaction / Imperial Data-Core

- **Key Event:** Prelate Vane initiates "Protocol: Silence," which overrides Warden safety protocols and authorizes lethal force city-wide to suppress the broadcast's "virus of truth."
  - _Character Defining Moment:_ **Prelate Vane** — He reveals his total lack of regard for human life, viewing mass murder as a "necessary cure" for the preservation of order.
  - _Key Dialog:_ **Prelate Vane:** "Initiate Protocol: Silence."

---

### Scene 2: The Massacre / Sector 4 Square

- **Key Event:** Acting under absolute orders, Wardens open fire on a crowd of civilians watching the broadcast, turning a moment of revelation into a public execution.
  - _Character Defining Moment:_ **The Wardens** — Their transition from guardians of the Zenith to its indiscriminate executioners is completed as they target men, women, and children.
- **Key Event:** Ria watches a Zenith Warden beat a child who looks like her brother Jax. The facade cracks. She realizes she has become the thing that killed her brother. She crushes the Warden's skull with her bare hands.
  - _Character Defining Moment:_ **Ria** — She switches sides not for Angelica, but for Jax. The soldier dies. The sister is reborn.
  - _Key Dialog:_ **Ria (whispered):** "Not again. Never again."

---

### Scene 3: The Last Drop / Bar, Sector 4

- **Key Event:** Wardens storm the "Last Drop" bar; Moxie sets the establishment on fire to distract the soldiers and allow her patrons to escape through the back.
  - _Character Defining Moment:_ **Moxie** — She shows heroic selflessness by burning the only home and business she has ever known to save the "rats" she cares for.
  - _Key Dialog:_ **Moxie:** "Closing time!"

---

### Scene 4: The Horror / Tower Roof

- **Key Event:** Watching the city-wide bloodbath from the tower roof, Angelica collapses under the psychological weight of the carnage her broadcast unleashed.
  - _Character Defining Moment:_ **Angelica Ward** — She struggles with the crushing moral burden of leadership, questioning if the price of truth is worth the lives being lost.

---

### Scene 5: The Resolve / Tower Roof

- **Key Event:** Zephyr hauls Angelica back to her feet, arguing that the people were always dying under the Founders and that the truth now gives them a chance to fight back.
  - _Character Defining Moment:_ **Zephyr** — He serves as Angelica’s moral anchor, pushing her to move past her guilt and embrace her role as the leader of the final war.
  - _Key Dialog:_ **Zephyr:** "You started a fire, Princess. You don't get to cry about the smoke. You have to burn the rest of the house down."
  - _Key Dialog:_ **Angelica:** "The truth didn't set them free. It just armed them. Now we have to lead them."
