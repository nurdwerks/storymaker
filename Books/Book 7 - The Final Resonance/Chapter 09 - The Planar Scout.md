## Chapter 09: The Planar Scout

### Scene 1: Exterior Hull / Sector 4

- **Key Event:** The separation of the shard from the main Sphere.
- **Detailed Description:** The massive, matte-black Sphere that hangs off the bow of the *Aeterna* does not launch a projectile in the traditional sense. Instead, a small, triangular section of its surface simply detaches. There is no thruster burn, no mechanical separation, and no sound transmission through the vacuum. The Shard acts as a perfect, frictionless glider. It is a two-dimensional object moving through three-dimensional space, which makes it appear to flicker in and out of existence depending on the viewing angle. When viewed edge-on, it is infinitely thin, effectively invisible. When it turns, it is a flat, vantablack triangle that absorbs all starlight. It rotates slowly, spiraling toward the ship's hull near Maintenance Airlock 4, tracking the electromagnetic emissions of the ship’s waste heat regulators.
- **Internal Conflict:** The ship’s automated defense systems fail to lock onto the object because it lacks sufficient mass or thermal signature to trigger the targeting algorithms. The AI registers it as a "sensor ghost" or a glint of starlight, ignoring the threat until it is physically touching the hull.

- **Key Event:** The Shard lands on the hull near Maintenance Airlock 4.
- **Detailed Description:** The landing is silent. The Shard does not impact the metal; it settles upon it like a shadow. As soon as it makes contact, the localized gravity plating in that section of the hull fluctuates. Frost patterns instantaneously form in perfect geometric spirals around the landing site, despite the vacuum. The Shard remains motionless, adhering to the metal surface not by magnetism, but by altering the local atomic bonds of the hull plating, effectively fusing with the *Aeterna* on a molecular level.

### Scene 2: Maintenance Airlock 4

- **Key Event:** Maintenance Crew Unit 4 deploys to investigate the hull anomaly.
- **Detailed Description:** The four-member team, led by Senior Tech Jenson, exits the airlock in standard EVA suits. They are expecting a micrometeorite impact or a hull fracture. They move clumsily in the zero-G environment, using magnetic tethers. Their comms are filled with routine chatter and complaints about the cold. Jenson approaches the "shadow" on the hull, his flashlight beam passing over it. The light does not reflect; it is swallowed. He reaches out with a gloved hand to touch the surface, assuming it is a burn mark or a carbon scorch.
- **Internal Conflict:** Jenson feels a primal wave of nausea as he approaches, a biological warning system reacting to the presence of "wrong" geometry. He hesitates, his hand trembling, but his training overrides his instinct.

  - _Character Defining Moment:_ **Tech Jenson** — He prioritizes the job over his survival instinct, a fatal error born of routine.
  - _Key Dialog:_ **Jenson:** "It's not a breach. It looks like... a stain. I'm going to scrub it."

- **Key Event:** The Shard activates, emitting a pulse of "Planar Collapse" light.
- **Detailed Description:** The moment Jenson’s glove brushes the surface, the Shard expands. It does not explode outward; it expands *inward*, creating a field of white, null-light that encompasses the entire maintenance crew. The light is silent and cold. Inside the field, the laws of physics are rewritten. The Z-axis (depth) is instantaneously deleted from the local area. The 3D world in a 10-meter radius is violently flattened into 2D. The transition is too fast for the human brain to process as pain.
- **Internal Conflict:** The crew members do not have time to scream. Their neural impulses are flattened along with their neurons.

- **Key Event:** The crew members are compressed into 2D diagrams.
- **Detailed Description:** The white light fades, revealing the aftermath. The four crew members are no longer standing. They have become detailed, anatomically correct diagrams "painted" onto the hull floor. The image is hyper-realistic but utterly flat. Every organ, every bone, and every circuit in their suits is visible in cross-section, laid out with impossible precision. There is no blood, as the fluids have been compressed into the same plane as the solids. They look like technical schematics of human beings. They are dead instantly, their biology rendered into geometry. The "stain" on the hull is now a gruesome mural of the maintenance team.

### Scene 3: The Monitor Room

- **Key Event:** Ria watches the feed from the external cameras.
- **Detailed Description:** In the security hub, Ria stands before a wall of monitors. The feed from Airlock 4 shows the flash of white light, and then the static image of the "paintings." She rewinds the footage, playing it back frame by frame. She watches the men collapse, not by falling down, but by folding in on themselves like origami. The violence is antiseptic. There is no gore, no scattered limbs, just a sudden simplification of complex life into basic shapes. The horror comes from the cleanliness of the kill. It implies an enemy that does not hate them, but simply edits them.
- **Internal Conflict:** Ria feels a cold sweat break out under her armor. She is a soldier who understands kinetic warfare—bullets, explosions, blood. She can fight things that bleed. This "math" is outside her comprehension. She feels a deep, visceral revulsion, a rejection of the "unnatural" death she just witnessed. She realizes that her physical strength and heavy armor are meaningless against an enemy that can delete a dimension.

  - _Character Defining Moment:_ **Ria** — She vomits into a waste bin, her iron stomach turned by the sheer wrongness of the sight. She wipes her mouth, her hand shaking, then immediately clenches it into a fist to stop the tremor.
  - _Key Dialog:_ **Ria:** "They didn't just kill them. They... simplified them. It's like someone clicked 'flatten image' on a terminal."

- **Key Event:** Ria seals the bulkhead to Sector 4.
- **Detailed Description:** Recovering her composure, Ria slams her hand onto the lockdown panel. Blast doors slam shut across the sector, isolating the "infection." She knows she cannot send a medical team; there are no bodies to retrieve, only graffiti. She initiates a quarantine protocol usually reserved for hull breaches or viral outbreaks, marking the sector with a "BIO-HAZARD" warning, though she knows the label is inadequate.
- **Internal Conflict:** She feels a pang of guilt for abandoning the "bodies," knowing they are essentially stuck to the hull, exposed to space. It violates the soldier's code of "leave no man behind," but she knows there are no men left, only geometry.

  - _Key Dialog:_ **Ria:** "Sector 4 is sealed. No one goes out there. Not unless they want to be part of the wallpaper."
