# Character Profile: Prelate Vane (The Flesh Architect)

**Archetype:** The Mad Scientist / The Evolutionary Zealot **True Sin:** GLUTTONY (Not for food, but a ravenous, bottomless hunger for Essence, Data, and Evolution. He wants to consume the secrets of the universe, regardless of the indigestion it causes the rest of humanity). **Virtue Mask:** CHARITY (He frames his horrific experiments as "Gifts." He believes he is uplifting humanity from its weak, mortal flesh into something eternal. "I hurt you to save you").

## I. Physiognomy & Appearance: The Walking Autopsy

Vane is a man who has hollowed himself out to make room for his genius. He exists on the razor's edge of biological viability, kept alive only by the machinery he worships and the dark magic he studies.

### 1. The Skeletal Frame

- **Stature:** He is jarringly tall, standing at 6'6", but he is skeletal. He weighs perhaps 140 lbs soaking wet. His height emphasizes his emaciation; he looks like a mantis stretched over a rack.

- **The Skin:** His epidermis is translucent, possessing the texture of parchment paper or onion skin. Under the harsh lights of his lab, one can see the blue roadmap of his veins and the black, necrotic tracks where **Prima-Materia** (Protomolecule) has scarred his system. He bruises if touched too hard. He looks fragile, but it is the fragility of a pressurized glass vial—dangerous to break.

- **The Lack of Hair:** He is entirely hairless. No eyebrows, no eyelashes. This gives his face a reptilian, unreadable quality. Sweat beads roll unimpeded down his forehead, which he dabs away with a sterile cloth.

### 2. The Ocular Implants (The Windows to the Void)

Vane removed his own eyes years ago, deeming biological sight "inefficient" because it only perceives the visible light spectrum.

- **The Prosthetics:** In their place, sitting in raw, red sockets, are **Multi-Spectral Oculars**. They are milky white orbs with no pupils. When he focuses, mechanical apertures shutter and click, zooming in or shifting spectrums.

- **The Gaze:** He sees thermal signatures, Resonance flows, and structural density. When he looks at a person, he doesn't see a face; he sees a heat map, a skeleton, and a caloric potential. He never blinks.

### 3. The Apparatus (The Life Support)

Vane is permanently fused to his technology. Strapped to his back, beneath his robes, is a brass and glass rig known as **The Hive**.

- **The Mechanism:** It is a tank of nutrient fluid containing the **Brood Queen** of his Nano-Swarm.

- **The Interface:** Clear plastic tubes run from the rig, piercing the skin of his neck and plugging directly into his jugular vein and carotid artery.

- **The Circulation:** You can watch his blood pump out of his body, cycle through the machine (feeding the Queen), and pump back in. He is physically tethered to his power source. The sound of the pump—_hiss-click, hiss-click_—follows him everywhere.

## II. Sartorial Style & Accessories

Vane dresses to disappear into the sterility of his work. His style is "Clinical Monastic."

- **The Robes:** He wears pristine, surgical white robes made of a self-cleaning hydrophobic polymer. Blood, oil, and bile slide off the fabric without staining. He is obsessed with external sterility to contrast the internal rot of his body. The robes have deep, dimensional pockets (Resonance-expanded) containing specimen jars, scalpels, and data-wafers.

- **The Gloves:** He wears transparent latex gloves that fit like a second skin. He changes them five or six times a conversation. The snapping sound of him pulling on a fresh pair is a psychological trigger for his victims—it means work is about to begin.

- **The Footwear:** Silent, white rubber-soled shoes. He moves without sound, gliding like a ghost or a hospital orderly.

- **The Accessory:** **The Laser-Scalpel.** It is an antique medical tool modified with a Resonance beam. He holds it like a conductor's baton. He uses it to gesture, to point, to eat, and to dissect. It is an extension of his index finger.

## III. Mannerisms, Ticks, and Psychological Quirks

Vane is defined by his detachment. He views reality as a simulation he is debugging.

- **The Self-Cannibalism (The Feeding):** This is his most horrific tick. His Nano-Swarm requires organic biomass to reproduce. When he needs to use magic, or when the Queen is hungry, Vane will casually slice off a piece of his own body—a finger tip, a patch of skin from his arm, an earlobe—and feed it to the swarm. He uses rapid-healing stims to grow it back, but the act is casual, conversational auto-cannibalism. It demonstrates his total lack of attachment to his physical form.

- **Data Narration:** He narrates his life in real-time for his audio logs. He speaks in the third person or the passive voice. Instead of saying "I am angry," he will mutter, "Subject Vane displays elevated cortisol. Aggression response triggered. Interesting." He dehumanizes everyone, including himself, into data points.

- **Invasion of Personal Space:** He has a compulsion to touch the pulse points of people he talks to. He will reach out with cold, latex-covered fingers and press them against a student's neck or wrist mid-sentence. He claims it is "medical concern," but it is a dominance display. He wants to feel their fear.

- **The Lick:** He suffers from chronic dehydration due to the blood-cycling of his apparatus. He constantly runs his tongue over his dry, cracked lips. It gives him a hungry, predatory look.

## IV. The Bond: Legion and The Biomass Toll

Vane’s magic is a perversion of nature. It is technology trying to be biology, and biology trying to be a machine.

- **The Beast:** **Legion** (The Nano-Swarm).

  - **Appearance:** A cloud of millions of microscopic mechanical insects. Individually, they look like dust motes. Together, they form a shimmering, black cloud that moves with fluid dynamics. They buzz with the sound of a thousand dental drills.

  - **Personality:** The Swarm has no personality, only hunger. It is a hive mind driven by the Queen on Vane’s back. It loves Vane the way a parasite loves a host.

- **The Mechanics:**

  - **Overt Manifestation:** The Swarm can solidify into shapes—shields, spears, surgical tools. They can strip a human body to clean bone in under thirty seconds.

  - **Covert Manifestation:** Vane can inhale the swarm or inject them into others. They act as spies (hearing/seeing inside a target) or internal torture devices (eating organs from the inside out).

- **The Sacrifice:** **Biomass.**

  - _The Cost:_ Energy cannot be created or destroyed. To create a swarm large enough to defend himself, Vane must provide raw organic material.

  - _The Horror:_ He is constantly eating high-calorie nutrient paste to maintain his weight, yet he is always starving because he feeds his mass to the bugs. He is slowly eating himself to death to fuel his ambition.

## V. Backstory & Pre-Narrative Life

Vane was not born a monster; he was made one by the stagnation of the Zenith.

**The Academic:** Vane was once a brilliant biologist in the Arcanum. He realized that the Zenith gene-pool was degrading due to inbreeding and over-reliance on comfort. He predicted the species would go extinct within five generations.

**The Rivalry:** His rival, **Archon Solon**, argued for "Natural Evolution." Vane argued for "Forced Evolution." Vane believed humanity needed a predator to evolve. When the Council sided with Solon, Vane snapped. He realized he had to _become_ the predator.

**The Discovery:** Vane discovered the **Prima-Materia** (Protomolecule) in the ship's waste drives. He realized it wasn't just fuel; it was mutagenic DNA. He began experimenting on himself, replacing his eyes and bonding with the Swarm. He framed Solon’s murder (Book 2) not out of petty jealousy, but because Solon was "standing in the way of the future."

**The Philosophy:** Vane believes in **Charity**. He truly thinks he is the most generous man alive. He is willing to do the ugly work—the torture, the vivisection, the culling—so that the human race can become strong enough to survive the dark. He views morality as a luxury that a dying species cannot afford.

## VI. Narrative Arc & Character Growth

### Book 1: The Iron Selection — **_The Talent Scout_**

- **State:** Vane is the bored administrator of the Selection. He expects another year of mediocre disappointments.

- **The Shift:** He meets **Angelica**. When he stress-tests her, he sees the anomaly. She is the first human he has met who metabolizes fear into power. He becomes obsessed.

- **The Action:** He manipulates the Games not to kill her, but to temper her. He throws obstacles in her path (like the Chimera) to see how she adapts.

- **The Climax:** When Angelica triggers "The Silt Broadcast," Vane is ecstatic. He overrides the execution order because he realizes she is the "Alpha Subject." He covers up the Headmaster's death to secure her loyalty (and debt).

### Book 2: The Cipher of Sins — **_The Conspirator_**

- **State:** Vane realizes the Emperor is a dead end. He needs a new figurehead.

- **The Plot:** He frames Angelica to force her out of the Academy and into the wild, testing her survival skills in the Mid-Levels.

- **The Encounter:** In the Museum, he demands the **Gene-Key**. When he realizes she is the Sangreal (Captain's Blood), his Gluttony spikes. He doesn't want to serve her; he wants to harvest her bone marrow to synthesize the Command Voice for himself.

- **The Alliance:** He allies with her at the end, offering her the throne. He thinks he can control her. He underestimates her Pride.

### Book 3: The Game of Gears — **_The Butcher_**

- **State:** As Court Mage, Vane is unleashed. He begins "The Forging."

- **The Horror:** He creates the **Forged**—Undercity citizens injected with Prima-Materia. They explode, turn into monsters, or die screaming. Vane records it all with clinical detachment.

- **The Interaction:** Angelica confronts him. Vane explains, "You are the prototype, my dear. I am simply trying to mass-produce you." He reveals he views her not as a person, but as a product.

- **The Survival:** When the Palace collapses, Vane shields himself with a swarm-cocoon. He survives by eating the bodies of the fallen courtiers trapped in the rubble with him.

### Book 4: The Stone Season — **_The Survivor_**

- **State:** In the apocalypse, Vane thrives. The polite mask drops.

- **The Descent:** He hoards warmth and food. He uses his magic to stay clean while others wallow in filth.

- **The Feast:** When they run out of food, Vane feeds his own left arm to the Swarm to keep them combat-ready, then uses magic to regrow it slowly. The group watches this auto-cannibalism in horror.

- **The Hive Mind:** He is fascinated by **The Mother**. He wants to study her, not kill her. He argues that the Hive Mind is the next logical step of evolution.

### Book 5: The Silent Void — **_The Transhumanist_**

- **State:** On the Ship, Vane discovers the pure **Prima-Materia** tanks.

- **The Transformation:** He stops eating food. He starts injecting the alien matter directly. His skin turns blue. He stops needing to breathe. He is becoming a new species.

- **The Hack:** When Angelica takes the Chair, Vane tries to hijack her mind. He attempts to upload his consciousness into the ship, believing that he should be the brain of the new world. He fails, purged by Angelica’s will, but his consciousness survives as a digital virus.

### Book 6: The Architect of Time — **_The Wraith_**

- **State:** Vane exists as a "Time-Wraith"—a non-corporeal data ghost haunting the past.

- **The Goal:** He possesses **Ragnar**, the barbarian king. He merges his future-tech knowledge with Ragnar’s brute strength.

- **The Dynamic:** He taunts Angelica in her dreams. "You cannot kill an idea, Angelica. I am Evolution." He tries to stop the ship launch to create a paradox where humanity stays on the planet and he rules as a God-King.

### Book 7: The Final Resonance — **_The Deleted File_**

- **State:** Vane manages to reconstitute a body in the future using the ship's bio-printers. He is now a grotesque hybrid of man, machine, and alien goo.

- **The Climax:** He interrupts the negotiation with **The Entity**. He demands The Entity give _him_ the power of Order.

- **The End:** The Entity looks at Vane. It sees his Gluttony—the endless, pointless hunger. The Entity deems him "Corrupted Data." It doesn't fight him; it simply deletes him. Vane dissolves into static, screaming that he is necessary. He dies not with a bang, but with a formatting error.

## VII. Relational Deep Dives

### 1. With Angelica (The Creator and The Monster)

- **The Dynamic:** _Frankenstein and the Creature._

- **Complexity:** Vane is the closest thing Angelica has to a mentor. He taught her how to survive. He taught her that sentiment is a weakness.

- **The Mirror:** By Book 5, Angelica realizes she is making decisions based on Vane’s logic ("The many outweigh the few"). She hates him because she sees him in the mirror. He loves her because she validates his life’s work.

- **The End:** Angelica’s rejection of him is a rejection of cold logic. She chooses messy humanity over his perfect evolution.

### 2. With Dr. Aris (The Academic Feud)

- **The Dynamic:** _The Butcher vs. The Observer._

- **Complexity:** Aris (Pride) believes he is smarter than Vane. Vane (Gluttony) believes he is bolder than Aris.

- **The Conflict:** Aris wants to communicate with the Aliens; Vane wants to dissect them. Aris treats the Virals as subjects; Vane treats them as raw materials.

- **The Resolution:** Vane views Aris’s death in the Gravity Well as "inefficient." He feels no loss, only annoyance that a good brain was wasted.

### 3. With The Entity (The False God)

- **The Dynamic:** _The Worshipper and the Idol._

- **Complexity:** Vane spends his life seeking the "Source" of the universe. When he meets the Entity, he thinks he has found God.

- **The Rejection:** The Entity rejects Vane. It values Order. Vane represents Cancer—uncontrolled growth and consumption. Vane’s realization that his God finds him disgusting is his final, breaking moment.

### 4. The Science Directorate

Vane is the smartest man in the room, and he surrounds himself with people who validate that fact. His circle is cold, clinical, and cruel.

- **Adjunct Professor Kroll:** A sycophant who laughs at Vane’s dry, intellectual jokes. He carries Vane’s tablet and echoes his sentiments. "Precisely, Prelate. The variable is flawed." He points his stylus at Angelica as if she is an equation that doesn't balance.

- **Dr. Splicer:** A genetics expert who is fascinated by Vane’s "Vision." They discuss eugenics over lunch. They look at Angelica and discuss her "breeding potential" and "genetic yield" as if she isn't there. It makes Angelica feel like livestock.

- **The Archivist:** A cybernetic construct that records Vane’s legacy. It hovers over his shoulder. It ignores Angelica completely, as she is "temporary data," while Vane is "historical significance."

- **Lab Assistant Igor:** Not his real name, but a hunchbacked, eager assistant who fetches Vane’s samples. He worships Vane. He hisses at Angelica if she touches any equipment. "Master’s tools! Do not touch!"
