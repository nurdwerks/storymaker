# Character Profile: The Jeweler

**Archetype:** The Craftsman / The Enabler
**True Sin:** PRIDE (He views the biological as raw material for his "art," and himself as the judge of quality).
**Virtue Mask:** PERFECTIONISM (He claims his cruelty is just "Professional Standards").

## I. Role in the Story

The Jeweler is a master craftsman who is constantly measuring Lady Vesper for new spinal braces, jeweled collars, and razor-sharp talons. He treats Vesper as a work of art and everyone else as raw, dirty material.

- **The Perspective:** He treats Vesper with religious reverence and Angelica with clinical disgust.
- **The Interaction:** He is fiercely protective of Vesper's "composition," snapping at Angelica for even getting near her. "Do not touch the Lady," he warns. "Your hands are greasy."

- **Event Location:** [Book 3, Chapter 23: The Slaughter](file:///c:/Users/bryan/Documents/storymaker/Books/Book%203_%20The%20Game%20of%20Gears%20%28Original%20Terms%29.md#L1821-1828)
- **Defining Quote:** (Witnessing his own work destroy her) "The 'improvements' he had made to her bone density were now her undoing." (Reveals flaw: PRIDE)
