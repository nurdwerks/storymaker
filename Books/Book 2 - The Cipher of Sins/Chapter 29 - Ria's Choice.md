## Chapter 29: Ria's Choice

### Scene 1: The View / Street level, Sector 4

- **Key Event:** Ria (Officer 884) witnesses a fellow Warden execute a child holding a toy robot; the act triggers a "core logic violation" in her cybernetic programming.
  - _Character Defining Moment:_ **Ria** — Her deep-seated conditioning as a "protector" finally shatters when faced with the undeniable reality of the Wardens as monsters.

---

### Scene 2: The Snap / Street level

- **Key Event:** Reclaiming her childhood memories of being rescued by a Warden, Ria recognizes that the regime she serves has become the very evil it once promised to fight.
  - _Character Defining Moment:_ **Ria** — She makes a conscious choice to reject her identity as "Officer 884" and reclaim her humanity as a free agent.
  - _Key Dialog:_ **Ria:** "Not an error. A correction."

---

### Scene 3: The Turn / Street level

- **Key Event:** Ria rams her Interceptor bike into the child’s murderer and removes her helmet, publicly identifying herself as "Ria" and drawing her blade to defend the civilians.
  - _Character Defining Moment:_ **Ria** — She fully defects from the Wardens, transitioning from an enforcer of the regime to its most dangerous internal enemy.
  - _Key Dialog:_ **Ria:** "I am not 884. I am Ria. ... And I am defending this child."

---

### Scene 4: The Riot / Street level

- **Key Event:** Ria’s defection inspires the cowering citizens and gangs to fight back; she becomes the "unlikely general" of a massive Undercity uprising that drives the Wardens back.
  - _Character Defining Moment:_ **Ria** — She embraces the raw, righteous anger of the people, leading a primal charge that turns a massacre into a revolution.
  - _Key Dialog:_ **Ria:** "I'm not a hero. I'm just pissed off. Who wants to break something?"
